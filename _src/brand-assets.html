Logo Evolution

The foundation and concept of the Browsersync logo (fig 1) was to demonstrate Browsersyncs awesome testing ability across different devices, screen sizes and web browsers. It was the a case of refining this concept into something simple, legible and memorable (fig 2-4).

Browsersync name

Browsersync (previously BrowserSync) is now one word, capital B, and nothing else. This was changed to stop the abbreviation "BS". No BS, just Browsersyn.

Typeface

Titillium has been select for its modern and technical appearance and is used for the logo type and heading. Open sans was selected for its friendly appearance and used for body text. Our fonts characteristics represent Browsersync's technical but friendly nature.

Logo Usage

To increase legibility it is important to have a strong contrast between the logo and background, for light backgrounds use the primary red and dark backgrounds white. If on top of image overlays or mid tone colours use your best judgment to use either red or white and create the highest contrast possible.
